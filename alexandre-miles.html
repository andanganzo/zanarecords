Alexander Miles

Alexander Miles was born on May 18, 1838 in Duluth, Minnesota, died on May 7, 1918 (aged 80)

He is known for his contribution of the elevator industry, was an African-American inventor of the late 19th century who transcended racial barriers in the United States. He designed an elevator that was able to open and close its own doors and the elevator shaft doors. learn more
